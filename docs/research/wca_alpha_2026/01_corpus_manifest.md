## 4. Excluded as off-topic (the low-relevance bulk)

Of 231 triaged items, 173 scored 0-3 and were set aside. The single largest cluster is the **`github resources/Curvy-CUSIPs` repository dump** (135 files): fixed-income / rates research notes, sell-side decks, yield-curve construction and bootstrapping papers, swaption/vol primers, and PCA-for-rates pieces. These cover an unrelated asset class (US Treasuries, swaps, MBS, inflation-linkers) and, while a handful touch transferable techniques (Kalman filtering, state-space, PCA, Gaussian processes), the framing is rates-specific with little transfer to bracket combinatorics or sports forecasting. A few general-quant items inside that repo (Gaussian Processes & Bayesian Optimization, GP regression, MARSS state-space) scored higher and appear above or in the mid tier.

Other excluded clusters:
- **Self-help / general non-fiction** (e.g. *Conquer Your Campus*, *Get Out of Your Own Way*, *Getting Past Your Past*, *This Naked Mind*, *Sapiens*, *Tao Te Ching*, *Just for Fun*): no quantitative content.
- **Finance narrative / true-crime / memoir** (*Black Edge*, *Liar's Poker*, *When Genius Failed*, *Traders Guns & Money*): readable but no transferable methods.
- **Unrelated asset-class derivatives & valuation** (commodities, exotic options, fixed income, M&A/LBO, technical analysis, trend-following): pricing/valuation machinery that does not bear on the WCA program.
- **Intro-level / generic programming** (*Python for Dummies*, *Python All-In-One*, generic data-science handbooks, QuantLib cookbooks): superseded by the stronger quant-Python and stats references retained in the mid/high tiers.

These remain in the corpus for completeness and occasional reference but are out of scope for the active research phases.
