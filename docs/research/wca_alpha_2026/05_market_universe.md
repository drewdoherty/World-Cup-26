# 05 — Market Universe & Model-Readiness Matrix

**Scope.** The remaining-knockout market universe for the 2026 World Cup (R32 leftovers → Final), each market's *settlement identity*, the wca module that prices it, the data feed that would test it, the arb hedge key, and a one-word readiness verdict. The readiness matrix is the centrepiece. It is followed by the settlement traps (the failure modes the matrix exists to defend against) and the forecasting benchmark + calibration / uncertainty / validation plan.

> **STALLED-FEED CAVEAT (read first — it gates almost everything below).**
> The OddsAPI `h2h` / `totals` / `btts` **book feed is stalled at 2026-06-23** (7 days old; R32 is already underway and four R32 ties are decided). The per-fixture cache (`site/scores_data.json`, 11 R32 fixtures) carries **only Polymarket-derived 1X2/totals/BTTS mids — no sportsbook/exchange book prices.** By contrast the **Polymarket advancement feed is fresh to 2026-06-29** (`pm_snapshots`). This split freshness means: **the model side is largely ready; the *price* side for every 90-minute book market is stale or absent.** Pairing a stale book leg against a fresh PM leg (or against an already-resolved fixture) manufactures phantom edges. The core arb detectors in `wca.arb` do **not** timestamp-gate, so **stale book legs must be excluded manually.** Every "partial"/"blocked" verdict in the matrix below traces to this caveat, not to a model gap.

**One structural takeaway.** The only genuinely *hedgeable* 90-min keys the engine will pair are `1x2_90min`, `btts_90min`, `dnb_90min`, `totals_<line>_90min`. Everything ET/pens-inclusive (advancement, outright, finalist) returns `settlement_key() = None` **on purpose** and is traded as **model-vs-PM edge only**, sized by quarter-Kelly in `advancement.compare_to_polymarket` — never as a cross-venue arb. The stalled book feed currently blocks the hedgeable set from being evaluated live, leaving the **fresh PM advancement surface as the single actionable remaining-bracket edge**.

## 2. Settlement traps

The matrix's `settlement_key`/readiness logic exists to defend against these. They are ordered by how dangerous (and how easy to commit) they are.

1. **90-min-vs-ET/pens FAKE ARB (the headline trap).** A PM/book **90-min 1X2 has a DRAW outcome and settles on regulation time**; a **to-advance / reach-R16 market has NO draw and settles INCLUDING ET and penalties.** Pairing "back Team A to advance" against "back Draw + back Team B 90-min" *looks* like a 3-way partition but is **not mutually exclusive** — Team A can draw/lose in 90 yet still advance on pens. `arb.py` defends: `settlement_key()` returns `None` for any outright/to-qualify/advancement market, so `find_cross_book_arbs` and `find_pm_book_arbs` **refuse to form a leg** with them; only `1x2_90min`, `btts_90min`, `dnb_90min`, `totals_<line>_90min` can pair.

2. **TO-ADVANCE vs MATCH-WIN.** "Team A to win the match" (90-min, draw loses) ≠ "Team A to advance" (wins on pens still pays). In a knockout **P(advance) > P(win in 90)** by the ET/pens mass. **Never substitute** the advancement-model prob (`advancement.py` `reach[]`, ET-inclusive by construction) for a 90-min 1X2 prob, or vice-versa.

3. **DNB / AH 0.0 refund-on-90-min-draw.** Draw-No-Bet and AH 0.0 **VOID the stake on a 90-min draw even if that team advances on penalties.** Do not treat a DNB win-side as equivalent to to-advance.

4. **PLAYER-PROP VOID ON NON-PARTICIPATION.** Anytime/first scorer, to-be-booked, SOT/shots/fouls all **VOID if the player doesn't start/appear.** Knockout rotation, suspensions (yellow-card accumulation reset rules), and injuries make participation uncertain; an un-voided model prob (`scorers.py` / `betbuilder.py` assume `expected_minutes`) **overstates EV.** Stake is returned not lost, so the realised distribution is a mixture the flat model prob ignores.

5. **FIRST-GOALSCORER own-goal & substitution rules.** Own goals don't count toward first/anytime; a player coming on after the first goal can't win first-scorer. `scorers.prob_first_scorer` is flagged a slightly generous approximation that ignores minutes non-homogeneity — **do not arb it** against a book first-scorer price.

6. **CARDS / CORNERS ET-inclusion varies by book.** Most card/corner totals are 90-min but **some books settle including extra time.** Two "identical" lines at different books can have different settlement windows → a fake arb. There is **no arb `settlement_key` for cards/corners** (returns `None`), so the engine won't pair them; **never hand-pair them.**

7. **PENALTY-IN-MATCH vs SHOOTOUT.** "A penalty in the match" = a penalty kick awarded in 90 min (usually + ET), a **different event** from the post-ET shootout. Conflating them, or treating shootout goals as match/scorer goals, mis-settles totals, BTTS, scorer and penalty markets (90-min totals/BTTS **exclude ET and the shootout entirely**).

8. **CORRECT-SCORE / TOTALS / BTTS are REGULATION-TIME.** All goal-count markets (CS, O/U, BTTS) settle on 90 minutes; ET and shootout goals excluded. A tie that goes to ET can have a **90-min 0-0** (BTTS No, Under, 0-0 CS all win) while still producing a "winner" via pens — **the goal markets and the advancement market resolve on disjoint information.**

9. **STALE/MIXED-FRESHNESS DATA TRAP (operational).** The OddsAPI 1X2/totals/btts book feed is stalled at 2026-06-23 while the PM advancement feed is fresh to 2026-06-29 and several R32 ties are decided. Pairing a stale book price against a fresh PM price (or an already-resolved fixture) **manufactures phantom edges.** The `accas` promo-freshness gate (6h) and the finished-fixture filter suppress these, but **the core arb detectors do not timestamp-gate**, so stale book legs must be excluded manually. (This is the stalled-feed caveat, restated as a trap.)

## 4. Calibration plan (conditional knockout/advancement probabilities)

Strict **time-ordered, zero look-ahead**, mirroring the `halflife.md`/`blend_weights.md` fit discipline.

**Unit of evaluation — two layers.** (a) Single-match 1X2 and the derived **knockout-advance binary** (team-to-advance after 90'/ET/pens, since PM grades the advancer regardless of how); (b) multi-round advancement nodes (reach R16/QF/SF/F/Win) from `wca.advancement.run_advancement`.

**No look-ahead (hard rule).** Every probability scored on match M must come from models fit **only** on internationals dated strictly `< M`'s kickoff, with the DC decay `reference_date` pinned to that cutoff and the market consensus from a pre-kickoff `odds_snapshots` snapshot (read-only). For the conditional bracket, condition **only** on results already decided at the as-of date: group stage (repo) + the four settled R32 ties seeded fixed (M73 Canada, M74 Paraguay, M75 Morocco, M76 Brazil; Germany/Netherlands/Japan out). **Never** feed a later round's result into an earlier round's probability.

**Scoring rules (all three; reuse repo convention).**
1. **Log-loss** — multiclass nat-log for 1X2; binary for advance/eliminate. `wca.rigor.skill.log_loss` exists — reuse.
2. **Brier** — 3-class for 1X2 (sum over outcomes), binary for advancement. `ledger/reports.py` `calibration_report` already does binary Brier; **extend to 3-class** in the research script.
3. **RPS (Ranked Probability Score) — NEW, must be designed.** `RPS = 1/(r−1) · Σ_{i=1}^{r−1} (Σ_{j≤i}(p_j − o_j))²` over ordered categories. For 1X2 order outcomes {home win, draw, away win} (ordinal in goal-difference space) so home-win-predicted-as-draw is penalised less than predicted-as-away-win. RPS is the standard football metric (Constantinou & Fenton) and the right **primary** metric here. **Lead on RPS for 1X2; log-loss/Brier for the binary advance node.**

**Reliability bins (binary advancement/knockout).** Equal-width (repo default = 5 bins) **and**, because n is small, equal-count (quantile) bins. Per bin: n, mean predicted prob, observed frequency, **Wilson 95%** on observed rate (rigor standardises on Wilson — reuse, never bare points). Tabulate reliability (predicted vs observed), report **ECE** `= Σ_bin (n_bin/N)·|obs−pred|` and a logistic calibration **slope/intercept** via `wca.rigor.skill.calibration` (slope CI should contain 1, intercept CI contain 0).

> **Calibration is REPORT-ONLY, never gated green.** The G5 gate withholds a pass below `N_CALIB_MIN=100` settled outcomes (`src/wca/rigor/skill.py`). The 2026 knockout set is **~31 total knockout matches (16+8+4+2+1), ~24 still to play — far below 100.** State this explicitly: reliability + ECE + slope/intercept are reported but **never** green-gated.

**Walk-forward protocol.** Score in tournament time order: as each still-to-play R32 tie resolves (France–Sweden, Ivory Coast–Norway, … Australia–Egypt), append its (pred, outcome) to the running calibration set; **refit nothing on it before scoring.** Then R16 (Jul 4–7), QF (Jul 9–11), SF (Jul 14–15), Final (Jul 19). Strictly OOS, time-ordered, no leakage.

**Benchmark attribution.** Compute all three scores for **each** model column (market-only, Elo, DC, deployed blend, BMA-design, venue-aware) on the **identical** match set — apples-to-apples, exactly as the half-life/weight holdouts were held fixed.

## 5. Uncertainty method (intervals on bracket / advancement probabilities)

Two independent sources, reported separately then combined.

**1. Monte-Carlo standard error (simulation noise).** `wca.advancement.run_advancement` runs `n_sims=20000`, fixed `rng_seed`. For node prob `p̂`, `SE = sqrt(p̂(1−p̂)/n_sims)`; 95% MC band `p̂ ± 1.96·SE` (Wilson for tail nodes near 0/1). At n=20000 worst-case SE ≈ 0.0035 (a 25% node is ±0.6pp). Drive negligible by raising `n_sims` to **100k** for the published bracket, report residual SE. **MC SE is a LOWER BOUND** — it captures only sampling within the sim, not parameter or model uncertainty.

**2. Parametric bootstrap of model parameters (the dominant uncertainty).** MC SE understates true uncertainty by conditioning on point-estimate Elo and DC params. Propagate:
- **DC** — resample the historical match set (block/cluster bootstrap on `match_id`) **or** draw from the asymptotic covariance (inverse Hessian if exposed, else resample-and-refit B=200–500), each replicate re-fitting DC under the half-life + strict pre-as-of cutoff → a full bracket sim.
- **Elo** — bootstrap ratings via the same historical resample (Elo is path-dependent → resample-and-refit, not closed-form).
- **Market** — bootstrap the de-vig consensus by **resampling bookmakers** (match-resampled, exactly as `blend_weights.md` Step 3 did 1000× → the [0.858, 1.153] log-loss CIs).

For each of B replicates: draw params → run the advancement sim (smaller per-replicate `n_sims`, e.g. 5k, since parameter spread dominates) → record each node's prob. Bootstrap distribution → 95% percentile interval per node. **Total interval via law of total variance:** `Var_total(p_node) ≈ Var_bootstrap(E[p|params]) + E[Var_MC(p|params)]`; report `sqrt` as node SE.

**3. Contest-level caveat.** The headline product `P(perfect bracket) = ∏ conditional per-tie advancement probs along the realised path`. Its interval is the **joint MC+bootstrap percentile of that product** (compute per replicate, take percentiles) — **do NOT multiply independent per-node CIs.** Expect a tiny number with a wide multiplicative band; report **order-of-magnitude with the bootstrap percentile interval, never a false-precision point.** Preserve uncertainty throughout; never claim profitability.

## 6. Remaining-match validation (honest forward-test) — and why it can barely self-validate

> **THE RIGOR CAVEAT (up front).** One tournament ≈ 1 cluster. `wca.rigor.clv.py` `n_eff_clusters` deflates correlated observations and `rigor/verdict.py` `assemble_verdict` hard-codes `futures_only → INSUFFICIENT_SAMPLE` ("futures resolve once; effective sample is permanently N≈1"). The bracket/advancement futures resolve **once.** A single 2026 run **cannot** produce a statistically significant verdict on the bracket calls. "We called the champion" is n_eff≈1 — **anecdote, not evidence.** Be explicit: **no green light is achievable here.**

**What we CAN honestly run now (minimal defensible set).**

- **A. Per-match OOS scoring (the only thing with real power).** The ~24 still-to-play knockout matches (12 R32 + R16/QF/SF/F) are individually scorable 1X2 events. Treat each as one paired observation (model vs Shin-market de-vigged price), score log-loss/Brier/RPS, run the existing paired test `wca.rigor.skill.skill_vs_market` (G4: per-fixture paired log-loss differential, one-sided t). With ~24 fixtures this has **weak but non-zero power**; report differential + p-value + n_fixtures and let G4 return its honest pass/None. Pair with `brier_skill` (BSS) and a calibration table (report-only, n≪100 so G5 stays None). **The single most defensible quantitative check available now.**
- **B. Label-shuffle placebo.** Fix the model probabilities; permute the realised outcomes across the ~24 matches **B=10,000** times; recompute the chosen metric (RPS / paired log-loss diff) each shuffle → null distribution. Report the actual score's percentile vs this null. If the real score is not in the tail, **skill is not demonstrated.** Tests "is the ordering informative" — complementary to A's "do we beat the market".
- **C. Consistency / no-leakage audit.** Confirm every scored probability was generated strictly pre-kickoff from a pre-as-of fit (re-run the fit with the date cutoff; assert no post-cutoff match entered training). Correctness check, not a skill test, but the cheapest defensible guarantee.
- **D. What we CANNOT do:** claim the bracket itself is validated; backtest venue-aware/BMA on 2026 with any power; treat "champion called correctly" as evidence. Advancement-node calibration is report-only.

**Minimal recommended run:** (A) paired per-match log-loss/Brier/RPS vs Shin-market over the still-to-play knockout matches via `wca.rigor.skill`, + (B) 10,000-iter label-shuffle placebo on RPS, + (C) leakage audit. **Expected and correct verdict given n: INSUFFICIENT_SAMPLE / INCONCLUSIVE** — which is the honest answer, and the battery is built to say exactly that.
