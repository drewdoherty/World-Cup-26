Exhibit 1 // How Far Back Does The Data Go? half-life 8y · Elo running

“How far back did you use? Feels like it’s only valid if the team is at least ~50% the same squad.”

Good instinct — and the model already encodes it. We hold every international since 1872 (49,477 matches), but nothing is weighted equally. Dixon-Coles applies an 8-year exponential half-life: a match 8 years ago counts half as much as one today, 16 years ago a quarter. There is no hard cutoff — influence fades smoothly. Separately, Elo is a running rating updated after every single match, so a team’s number already reflects its recent trajectory; old results are superseded, not re-counted. Your “~50% same squad” line maps almost exactly to the ~4–8 year window that dominates the fit. By the time a squad has fully turned over, those matches carry <50% weight and Elo has re-converged on the current side.
