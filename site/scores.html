Visuals // Borrowed From Klement — Structure & Geography opt-in · default OFF

Two ideas adapted from Joachim Klement's socio-economic World Cup model — built as opt-in priors, off by default. They are not deployed: the market already prices structure on liquid 1X2, and the historical backtest can’t test the prior (no minnows play in WC/Euro/Copa finals). They wait for live 2026 evidence.

A. Structural strength index — a prior for data-poor teams

Population × football culture, an inverted-U in GDP/capita (peaking ≈ $60k), plus a confederation offset (Hoffmann-Ging-Ramasamy 2002). It only ever shrinks low-data minnows toward a sensible target instead of the global mean — for data-rich sides the match likelihood swamps it. Note it underrates South-American powers (Brazil, Argentina) on GDP: the known blind spot of socio-economic models, and exactly why it is a prior, not a signal.

structural strength z · src/wca/models/structural.py · prior only, never a market signal

B. Host advantage, diluted & altitude-aware

A single host’s ~100 Elo-point edge is wrong for three co-hosts who are only at home in the group stage. The opt-in path dilutes the bonus and adds an altitude tax on sea-level visitors — biggest at Mexico’s Estadio Azteca (~2240 m). Knockouts roam, so they stay neutral.

src/wca/models/venues.py · factor 0.5 + 0.02 pts/m altitude tax over 1000 m gap · opt-in
